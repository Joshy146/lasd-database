Use of Force Directive

Order Number: UOF-2026-0616 // Effective: June 16, 2026

FORCE FRAMEWORK MANUAL

I. Purpose & Philosophy

Deputies are permitted to use only that force which is objectively reasonable, proportionate, and necessary under the totality of the circumstances to effectively bring an incident under control, while protecting the safety of the deputy and others. Reasonableness must be judged from the perspective of a reasonable officer on the scene, rather than with the 20/20 vision of hindsight.

III. LASD Force Categorization Matrix

Subject Behavior Authorized Tactical Response Options Administrative Reporting Controls
Compliant / Cooperative Professional command presence, verbal communication, escort positions. Non-reportable tracking log.
Passive Resistance Verbal directives, strategic perimeter containment manipulation. Log entry if simple manipulation needed without injury.
Active Resistance Control holds, joint locks, leverage takedowns, chemical agents (OC spray). Category I Force Report: Immediate supervisor review.
Assaultive Behavior Conducted Energy Devices (Taser), Less-Lethal munitions, batons to non-vital areas. Category II Force Report: Full command witness investigation.
Life-Threatening Threat Firearms, lethal tools, improvised measures to vital body areas. Category III Force Report: Immediate deployment of IAB, Homicide Bureau.

V. Strict Restrictions and Prohibited Actions

Carotid Restraints & Chokeholds Strictly prohibited unless the deputy is involved in a lethal combat scenario where death or great bodily injury is completely imminent.
Discharging at Moving Vehicles Firearms shall not be discharged at a moving vehicle unless the vehicle itself is being deployed as a lethal weapon and no structural escape step exists.
