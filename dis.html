Deputy-Involved Shooting (DIS)

Order Number: DIS-2026-0616 // Effective: June 16, 2026

CRITICAL EXECUTIVE MANDATE

Policy Summary

The integrity of the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department rests upon rigorous accountability, absolute operational transparency, and adherence to constitutional policing standards. This protocol mandates standard operating procedures following any incident where a Department member discharges a firearm during the performance of official duties.

2.0 Critical Incident Command & Field Sequences

Phase Mandated Action Protocols Responsibility Centers
1. Scene Security Isolate the discharge zone. Establish a physical perimeter utilizing barrier tape, and initiate a Crime Scene Entry Log. Involved / Responding Deputies
2. Medical Triage Render immediate trauma care (tactical combat casualty care / CPR) to any wounded individuals. All On-Scene Personnel
3. Notifications Immediate priority notification to the station Watch Commander, who contacts SHB, Homicide, and IAB. Watch Commander / Field Supervisor
4. Public Safety Statement Obtain a brief, verbal PSS from the involved deputy regarding direction of fire, outstanding suspects, and weapon counts. First On-Scene Supervisor

4.0 Evidence Control & Protections

Mandatory Separation

Involved and witness deputies shall be immediately separated and transported. No details may be discussed prior to recorded interviews.

Weapon Surrender

The involved weapon will be surrendered in its exact post-incident state (round in chamber) to investigators. A replacement weapon is issued.

Garrity Protections

Statements compelled under administrative threat of termination cannot be used against the employee inside a criminal court trial.
