Welcome to the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Office

As a member of the LASD, you are part of an essential team committed to protecting and serving the citizens of Los Angeles County. The Employee Handbook outlines the expectations, rules, and guidelines to ensure that all personnel uphold the highest values of professionalism, integrity, and public service.

I. General Policies

1. Mission Statement

The mission of the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Office is to protect and serve the community, maintain public order, and ensure safety and justice through effective law enforcement, community engagement, and dedicated public service.

2. Code of Conduct

All employees must maintain the highest standards of behavior and professionalism both while on duty and off duty. This strictly includes:

  • Absolute honesty and unwavering integrity at all times.
  • Respectful, clear, and professional communication with both the public and colleagues.
  • Strict adherence to legal, moral, and ethical standards in all situations.

II. Work Hours and Schedules

LASD operates 24 hours a week, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Shift schedules fluctuate based on organizational and departmental needs. Personnel are expected to report to their designated duties precisely on time and be fully prepared, equipped, and cleared for their shift.

Requirement Type Active Duty Quota
Minimum Time Commitment Log at least 2 total hours a week.

III. Uniform and Appearance Standards

Employees must wear their designated departmental uniform at all times while on duty. A deputy's uniform symbolizes state legitimacy, authority, and team unity. Non-compliance will directly lead to disciplinary action.

  • Headwear Regulations: Deputies are permitted to wear an approved LASD campaign hat.
  • Personal Grooming & Tattoos: Employees are expected to maintain a clean, professional appearance. Hair must be neatly groomed, and all visible tattoos are strictly required to remain below the neck-line.

VI. Training and Development

1. Basic Law Enforcement Training (BLET)

All new hires must successfully complete the initial BLET qualification program prior to field deployment. This academy training comprehensively covers:

Standard law enforcement, patrol procedures, and firearm safety/lethal force protocols.
Physical fitness, defensive tactics, and stamina building.
Official report writing, legal documentation, and booking procedures.
Continuing Education sessions occurring throughout the calendar year.
