Emergency Response Procedures

Order Number: TAC-2026-0616 // Effective: June 16, 2026

CRITICAL INCIDENT PROTOCOL

I. General Emergency Response Protocol

1. Deputy Safety & Tactical Awareness

Dynamic Threat Assessment: Deputies shall continuously assess the landscape for active ballistics, secondary explosive devices, or biochemical exposures. Use high-engine block vehicle placement for hardened ballistic cover. Maintain strict radio discipline on assigned tactical frequencies (L-TAC or C-TAC).

2. Initial Scene Assessment & Containment

Rapid Size-Up & Perimeters: Broadcast a concise size-up report to the communications center (SCC) immediately. Establish Inner (hot zone isolation) and Outer (public access isolation) perimeters instantly. Differentiate immediately between evolving active threats and static barricades.

II. Critical Incident Directives

1. Active Shooter / Mass Casualty Incident (ASMCI)

In accordance with LASD Active Shooter Response Doctrine, the primary objective is the rapid, aggressive neutralization of the active threat to mitigate loss of life. Do not delay entry to await specialized elements.

  1. Immediate Contact Element (ICE): Form a 2 to 4 deputy team, breach, and bypass casualties directly toward gunfire.
  2. Threat Neutralization: Use overwhelming movement to stop the active killer via arrest or deadly force.
  3. Rescue Task Force (RTF): Pair secondary units with LACOFD personnel to clear the warm zone and apply tourniquets.
  4. Casualty Collection Point (CCP): Route all victims into a hardened, protected location for medical staging.

IV. Incident Command System (ICS) Structure

All critical incidents managed by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department shall follow the National Incident Management System (NIMS) Incident Command Structure.

Incident Commander

First arriving unit on scene assumes command regardless of rank until relieved.

Operations Section

Directs tactical execution on the ground field levels.

Planning Section

Tracks resources and develops comprehensive action plans.

Logistics Section

Secures specialized equipment, sustenance, and comms.
