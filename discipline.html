Disciplinary Policies & Procedures

Order Number: DPCS-2026-0616 // Effective: June 16, 2026

Internal Review Protocol

Statement of Purpose

This administrative directive establishes the standard regulatory framework for progressive discipline within the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD). The Department mandates the highest degree of ethical integrity, professional conduct, and constitutional policing. This document standardizes violation classifications, establishes explicit operational steps for administrative remediation, and outlines structural penalties to ensure accountability, consistency, and public trust.

Section II: Violation Classification Levels

Level 1: Minor

Administrative or routine infractions that do not directly compromise public safety or tactical field operations.

Examples: Absenteeism/farming hours, minor uniform or grooming non-compliance, late administrative paperwork, passive insubordination without aggressive behavior.

Level 2: Serious

Violations that cause significant threat to the operational integrity, safety, or tactical alignment of the unit.

Examples: Falsification of operational logs/reports, out-of-policy use of force, unauthorized use of Department gear/vehicles/BWCs, active insubordination or defiance of a direct supervisory order.

Level 3: Major

Severe actions, illegal acts, or gross neglect that cause immediate liability, danger, or severe breach of public trust.

Examples: Criminal conduct (theft, assault, DUI), on-duty substance impairment, official corruption/bribery, civil rights violations under color of authority, gross negligence.

Critical Civil Service Appeals Note Permanent, non-probationary employees retain the right to contest any adverse administrative action. Appeals must be formally entered into the Department ticket system within seventy-two (72) hours of receiving official notification. Investigations are reviewed by the Internal Affairs Bureau (IAB).
