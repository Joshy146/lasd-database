Body-Worn Camera Policy

Order Number: BWC-2026-0616 // Effective: June 16, 2026

MANDATORY USE & STORAGE STANDARDS

Purpose & Scope

This directive establishes rigid guidelines for the mandatory deployment, activation, and management of Body-Worn Camera (BWC) simulation systems within the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department (LASD) operational framework. Because direct in-game technical feeds cannot automatically write to servers, all deployed units are required to utilize third-party screen recording software acting as digital equivalents of tactical hardware.

Technical Simulation Standards

Approved Software Platform Optimized Application & Utility Storage Protocol
Medal.tv Highly recommended for standard patrol units. Streamlined for rapid clipping and automatic cloud rendering. Cloud Database / Shareable URL Link
OBS Studio Advanced open-source utility. Ideal for high-definition recording and complex multi-audio track setups. Local Disk Storage / Manual Cloud Export
NVIDIA ShadowPlay Low-overhead background capture hardware engine restricted to laptops/desktops with NVIDIA processors. Local Directory / Fast-clip instant replay
Xbox Game Bar Native Windows architecture capture tool deployed as a primary fallback option during technical software failures. Local Windows Captures Directory

Operational Guidelines & Shift-End Logging

Software activation must definitively capture all traffic stops, vehicle pursuits, arrest procedures, and tactical crowd controls. Raw data captures must remain completely unaltered.

Critical Departmental Warning Any intentional tampering, failure to record mandatory field events, or unexplained deletion of operational video files will be treated as serious misconduct, resulting in immediate suspension or permanent termination of department credentials.
#BWC-LOGS SUBMISSION RULE: Every unit must upload, link, or register their shift footage into the designated #bwc-logs channel prior to logging off duty, noting date, callsign, badge number, and an incident index summary.
